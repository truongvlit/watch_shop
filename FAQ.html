Mechanical Watch
  1. Why did my watch stop?

    2. Mechanical watches are powered by the movement’s mechanical parts, not by a battery. This means that the owner of the watch is required to wind up the watch to keep it running by storing energy into the spring. If you need help winding your watch, click here. If the user forgets to wind the watch, the energy stored in the mainspring of the watch runs out and the watch stops working. To restart your watch, wind it fully by rotating the crown clockwise 30-40 turns and then set the time. If the watch fails to restart, please contact us via email at info@eoniq.co.

  2. How accurate are mechanical watches?

    3. The accuracy of a mechanical watch depends on the machinery, or the movement, inside it. Our different watch types use different movements, which have different levels of accuracy. This accuracy is described as a “rate” - the amount of seconds per day that a watch will gain or lose time.

    The Merlot Series: ± 45 seconds/day

    The Navigator, Pinot Blanc, Pinot Noir and Alster Series: -20 to +40 seconds/day

  3. How do I wind up my watch?

    4. To wind your mechanical watch, first push to the crown towards the case to its normal position, where rotating it does not move the hands. Then, with the watch positioned face upwards, rotate the crown of the watch clockwise approximately 30-40 full turns, or until the crown begins to strongly resist rotation. This should wind the watch fully.

  4. What is the difference between Automatic and Hand-wound watches?

    5. With a Hand-wound mechanical watch (a manual watch), the wearer must supply energy for the watch by physically winding the crown on a regular basis to power the watch. If you need help winding your watch, please click here.

    With an Automatic mechanical watch, the movement is fitted with an oscillating weight called therotor – meaning that the watch automatically winds and powers itself from the owner’s natural wrist movement. When the power reserve left in the watch runs down due to the watch not being worn, the watch stops running and needs to be re-set before wearing again.

  5. How long will the watch run for? How much power can the watch reserve?
    • The Navigator, Pinot Blanc, Pinot Noir and Alster series: up to 40 hours
    • The Merlot series: up to 36 hours
  6. Is there any maintenance required for the watch?

    7. For automatic mechanical watches, you should wind it at least once a week, though regular wear is often enough to maintain it. For hand-wound, manual watches, you should wind it daily. Try to avoid letting any mechanical watch sit inactive for more than a month. Minimal maintenance is needed for mechanical watches. We recommend having the watch serviced every 5-10 years by a qualified watchmaker.

  7. Can EONIQ service my watch?

    8. Sorry we are not doing this at this moment but, if you have any question about your watch, please send us an email at info@eoniq.co and we will try to get back to you with a quote.

  8. How can I make sure my EONIQ watch lasts a long time and stays in a good condition?

    9. In general, you should avoid exposing your watch to physical shocks, extreme changes in temperature and humidity, as well as protect it from water. To keep your watch clean, we recommend carefully cleaning the watch with a damp cloth from time to time. Make sure you keep your mechanical watch away from magnet to get rid from magnetization, this may cause defect to your watch.

    For maximum longevity, we also recommend having the watch serviced every 5-10 years by a qualified watchmaker to make sure the movement stays in a good condition.

  2. When I proceed to checkout, it says “Payment Error. Your card was declined.” What should I do?

    3. Please kindly make sure all information entered is 100% correct and there are no spelling errors. Please also avoid short forms of City, and do not include State information within the City input box. If the details are correct but the payment still cannot be made, the problem is likely caused by your specific bank or card.

    When we submit a charge to your bank, they have automated systems that determine whether or not to accept the charge. These systems take various signals into account, such as your spending habits, account balance, and card information like the expiration date and CVC.

    Since these signals are constantly changing, a previously successful card might be declined in the future. Even if all of the card information is correct, and you previously had a successful payment, a future charge can still be declined by a bank’s overzealous fraud systems.

    If all of the card information seems correct, it is best for you contact your bank, inquire for more information, and ask for future charges to be accepted.
